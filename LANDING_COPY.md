## FAQ

**Q1: How do you know what I should be paying for these tools?**

We maintain a database of current public pricing for the most common AI developer tools — Cursor, GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT Teams, Claude Pro/Teams, Codeium, Tabnine, Perplexity, and others. We pull list price, seat minimums, and annual vs. monthly rates. We don't have access to your negotiated contracts, so if you've got a custom enterprise deal, the numbers will be directionally right but not exact. For most teams at the Series A–B stage, you're on standard pricing anyway.

---

**Q2: What counts as "waste"? My engineers actually use these tools.**

Good question — usage and value aren't the same thing. We flag waste in three categories: (1) **seat waste** — seats purchased vs. seats that have logged in in the last 30 days, (2) **overlap waste** — tools with materially overlapping capabilities where you're paying twice, and (3) **plan waste** — teams on a higher tier than their usage patterns justify. An engineer using Copilot 10 minutes a week when Cursor is doing 90% of their AI work is a wasted seat, even if they technically "use" it.

---

**Q3: Why don't you need me to log in or connect my billing accounts?**

Because we don't need to. We're not pulling live data — we're asking you to self-report what you're paying and for how many seats. That's enough to run the audit and flag the obvious inefficiencies. Requiring OAuth or billing access would create friction and a security conversation most EMs don't want to have for a tool they haven't vetted yet. If you want a deeper audit with actual usage data, that's what the Credex consultation is for.

---

**Q4: What happens to my data after I submit?**

If you don't enter your email, nothing leaves your session. The audit runs in your browser and we don't store the results. If you do enter your email to get the full report, we store your tool configuration and estimated spend so the Credex team can prep for your consultation. We don't sell your data or add you to a drip campaign. One outreach, one conversation.

---

**Q5: My situation is more complicated than a simple form can capture — we have custom enterprise pricing, partial seats, and some tools on personal cards.**

Totally valid. The audit is designed for the common case: standard pricing, full seats, centralized billing. If your situation is messier, the numbers will be less precise, but directionally useful — you'll still see which categories of tool are eating the most budget. The form has a free-text field where you can add context (e.g., "Copilot is on a 20% enterprise discount"), and if you book a consultation, the Credex team can dig into the nuances with you directly.
