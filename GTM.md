# GTM.md — CredMaster Go-To-Market Strategy

---

## Who Is the Exact Target User

**Job title:** Engineering Manager or VP of Engineering

**Company stage:** Series A or B, 15–80 person company. Engineering team is 8–25 people. The company has moved past "default free tier" and is now paying real money for developer tooling, but hasn't yet built the operational muscle to audit it.

**Budget ownership:** They sign off on or directly approve SaaS subscriptions under ~$2K/month without CFO involvement. Above that, they need finance's blessing — and they don't want to have that conversation without hard numbers.

**The exact situation they're in:**
- Paying for 3–6 AI tools simultaneously (Cursor, Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT Teams, Codeium, Tabnine, Perplexity, etc.)
- Seats were added one by one as engineers requested them, never reviewed in aggregate
- They haven't done a tools audit since the team was half this size
- The finance team just sent a quarterly SaaS review spreadsheet and they're dreading it
- They feel vaguely guilty — "we should probably cut something" — but don't have the data to justify a decision, so they defer

**What they are NOT:** a solo founder, a 500-person company with a procurement team, or a CTO who delegates all tooling decisions.

## How to Get the First 100 Users in 30 Days with $0

### Week 1 — Seed with warm outreach (Days 1–7)

**Day 1 (Tuesday, 10am):** Post in Rands Leadership Slack `#tools` channel.
> "Built a free AI spend auditor this week — you enter your tools, seats, and team size, and it shows you where you're wasting money. No login. Instant results. Would love feedback from anyone managing AI tooling budgets."

**Day 2 (Wednesday):** DM 10 engineering managers you know personally. Not a blast — personalized, one sentence each: "Hey, I built something you might find useful. Takes 2 minutes. Would love your gut reaction."

**Day 3 (Thursday, 9am):** Post to `r/ExperiencedDevs`:
> **Title:** "I built a free tool to audit your team's AI tool spend — curious if the numbers match your gut"
> Short post, link the tool, explain what it does in 3 sentences. Do NOT make it a pitch.

**Day 4 (Friday):** Share in Software Lead Weekly Slack. Follow up on any Reddit comments from Day 3.

**Day 5–7:** Respond to every comment. Treat each response as a user interview.

**Goal by end of Week 1:** 50–80 visitors, 15–25 audits completed, 3–5 email captures.

---

### Week 2 — Hacker News (Days 8–10)

**Day 8 (Monday, 9am ET — optimal HN time):** Submit to HN as "Show HN":
> **Title:** "Show HN: Free AI spend auditor for engineering teams (no login, instant results)"
> **Comment:** Explain the one-week build context, what it does, and what you're trying to learn. Mention it's a Credex job application project — the transparency plays well with the HN crowd.

**Day 9–10:** Monitor and respond to every comment within the first 3 hours (critical for HN ranking). If it hits the front page, expect 500–2,000 visitors in 24 hours.

---

### Week 3 — Content distribution (Days 11–21)

**Day 11:** Write a short Twitter/X thread:
> "I built a free AI spend auditor for engineering teams in one week. Here's what I learned about how teams actually waste money on AI tools: [thread]"
> End with the link. Post at 9am PT.

**Day 14:** Reach out to 3 newsletters (TLDR Engineering, Software Lead Weekly, The Pragmatic Engineer) with a one-paragraph pitch. Offer the "built in one week" angle — that's a story, not just a link.

**Day 17:** Post a LinkedIn article: "Your engineering team is probably paying for 3 AI tools that do the same thing. Here's how to find out." Targeted at EMs. Link CredMaster at the end.

**Day 21:** Post a follow-up on Reddit with learnings: "I launched a free AI spend auditor 2 weeks ago — here's what the data says about how teams waste money on AI tools." Data posts perform well; anonymize and aggregate.

---

### Week 4 — Double down on what worked (Days 22–30)

Look at which channel drove the most qualified completions (audit + email), and do more of that. If HN worked, find more HN-style communities (Lobsters, DEV.to). If Reddit worked, find more subreddits.

**Goal by Day 30:** 100+ audits, 15+ email captures, 3+ consultation calls booked.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

A random indie founder cannot do what Credex can: **send this tool to 100 existing customers before any public launch.**

Credex already has relationships with CTOs and VPs of Engineering at AI-forward companies. These are people who trust Credex's judgment on infrastructure decisions. A cold email from a stranger asking them to try an audit tool gets ignored. The same message from their existing Credex rep gets a 40–60% response rate.

Additionally, Credex can:
- Bundle CredMaster audit results into existing customer conversations ("we ran this against your team's stack — here's what we found")
- Use sales calls to pre-populate audit results and walk through them live
- Leverage conference presence (infrastructure and AI conferences) with a "run your audit live at our booth" angle

No outside founder has this. This is the moat.
